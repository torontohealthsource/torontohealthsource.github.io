Do Peptide Companies Test Every Batch?

Short answer: most don't. Genuine per-batch testing is expensive, so many vendors test once (or rely on their supplier's single test) and show that same COA for everything after. Unless you can tie the certificate to your batch, "lab tested" often means "a batch was tested once," not "this vial was tested."

How batch testing actually works

A "batch" (or lot) is one production run. True per-batch testing means each lot gets its own fresh analysis with its own COA and lot number. That's the gold standard — and it's costly, which is exactly why it's rare. The cheaper, common shortcut is COA reuse: one good test stretched across many untested lots.

How to tell if YOUR vial was tested

  1. Lot number match: does the lot on your physical vial match the lot on the COA? (Many vendors don't even print one.)
  2. Recent, specific date: a per-batch COA is recent and tied to that run — not a single document dated long ago and reused.
  3. Portal verification: the result resolves on the lab's own portal, tied to that lot.
  4. Independent blind testing: the strongest proof — someone bought off-the-shelf and tested it, so the vendor couldn't cherry-pick the sample.

The takeaway

"Do they test every batch?" is the right question to ask a vendor directly. An open, specific answer (with per-lot COAs you can verify) is a strong sign; a vague "everything is lab tested" usually means one test doing a lot of work.
