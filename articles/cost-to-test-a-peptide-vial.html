How Much Does It Cost to Test Your Own Peptide Vial?

Short answer: Independent identity and purity testing of a single sample is a modest flat fee at third-party labs — cheap relative to the order it protects. The number itself carries a lesson: if a buyer can afford to test, a vendor selling thousands of vials certainly can.

What you're paying for

A typical test bundle covers identity (is this the molecule it claims to be?) and purity (how much of the sample is that molecule?). Some buyers also pay for quantification. It's a small, predictable cost per sample.

Why "too expensive to test" is a red flag

When testing is this affordable, a vendor that skips it isn't saving money — it's avoiding scrutiny. The cost excuse doesn't hold up against the math of selling at scale.

How to submit a sample

  • Pick an independent lab, not one affiliated with the vendor.
  • Buy the product like a normal customer (a blind purchase).
  • Compare the result against the vendor's own published COA.
